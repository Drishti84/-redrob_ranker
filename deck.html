Solution Overview
What we built: A two-phase ranker that splits the heavy work from the fast work. Before evaluation day, we precompute sentence embeddings and extract 30+ structured signals for all 100,000 candidates. At ranking time, we embed the JD locally, run a single matrix multiply to get cosine similarities across all 100K profiles, pass everything through a 5-signal weighted scorer, and write the final CSV. The whole online phase takes 29 seconds on a regular CPU with no network calls.
Why this is different from keyword matching: Traditional systems treat "FAISS" on a resume as a binary hit whether the person used it for 2 months as an intern or 3 years in a production retrieval system. Our scorer weights every skill by proficiency × duration × endorsements. We separately track how long someone has actually worked in AI roles (not just listed skills). We use 23 platform signals to check if they will actually respond and join. We apply a 0.40x penalty to profiles that clearly stuffed keywords onto a non-AI career. And we filter out around 65 profiles with impossible career timelines before scoring even starts. Every signal is structured and explainable.

JD Understanding & Candidate Evaluation
What we pulled out of the JD
Experience: 5 to 9 years, product company scale, shipped to real users
Vector search: Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant, Milvus, OpenSearch, Elasticsearch, FAISS
Embeddings: sentence-transformers, BGE, E5, OpenAI embeddings used in production
Evaluation: NDCG, MRR, MAP, A/B testing. Not just building pipelines but actually measuring them
LLM fine-tuning: LoRA, QLoRA, PEFT or learning-to-rank methods like XGBoost and LambdaRank
Location: India, Pune or Noida preferred, open to hybrid. Availability signals matter here
How we go deeper than keyword matching
Skill depth: Each skill match is scored by proficiency (beginner 0.4x up to expert 1.0x), how long they used it (under 6 months gets 0.5x, over 24 months gets full credit), and endorsement count adding up to 20% on top
Career evidence: We track ai_role_months separately from listed skills. Someone can claim FAISS but if they never held an AI title we see that
Semantic similarity: We embed the full JD and the full profile text and compute cosine distance using all-MiniLM-L6-v2 at 384 dimensions
Hireability: 23 platform signals including open to work status, notice period, response rate, interview rate, GitHub score, and days since last active

Ranking Methodology
Retrieve: All 100K candidates are embedded offline with all-MiniLM-L6-v2 (384-dim, L2-normalised). At rank time we embed the JD locally and run a single matrix multiply: (100K x 384) @ (384x1) gives cosine similarities for every candidate in 0.04 seconds. We skipped FAISS entirely. A pre-filter would have cut recall and missed candidates who deserved to be in the top 100.
Models and algorithms: sentence-transformers for embeddings, a weighted linear combination of 5 hand-crafted signal functions for scoring, and rapidfuzz for fuzzy skill name matching so that "HuggingFace" and "Hugging Face Transformers" are treated as the same thing. No black-box models anywhere in the pipeline.

Explainability & Data Validation
How we explain every ranking decision: Each candidate in the top 100 gets a recruiter-style reasoning note built entirely from structured features, no LLM involved. For example: "Senior AI Engineer at Apple with 5.9 years of experience, 5+ years in AI and ML roles, covers FAISS, NLP, and Python. Open to work, 30-day notice, 80% recruiter response rate, India-based, active GitHub score of 97, tier 1 institution." The tone adjusts by rank. Top 10 reads confidently. From rank 31 onwards we include honest flags like a long notice period or location outside India.
Zero hallucinations: We make zero LLM calls during ranking. Every fact in the reasoning note (title, company, years of experience, skill names, availability) comes directly from the structured feature dictionary we extracted at precompute time. If "FAISS" shows up in the reasoning, it means that skill exists in the candidate's skill list with more than zero months of usage. Nothing is generated or inferred.
Catching bad data before it reaches scoring: Our honeypot detector runs 5 checks: career date arithmetic that is off by more than 12 months, an expert-level skill listed with zero months used, years of experience that exceed the sum of all role durations by more than 5 years, job overlaps longer than 180 days, and five or more expert skills each with under 3 months of use. 65 profiles were flagged and excluded. On top of that, profiles with a non-AI job title and under 12 months of AI work history receive a 0.40x multiplier on their skill score to push keyword stuffers down the ranking.

System Architecture
🗂 precompute.py
Reads candidates.jsonl into features.py, which pulls out title classification, skill hits with proficiency and duration weights, months in AI roles, whether the person is consulting-only, product experience, average tenure, education tier, and all 23 platform signals. Then honeypot.py flags 65 impossible profiles. Finally all-MiniLM-L6-v2 embeds the profile text in batches of 256 and saves features.jsonl and embeddings.npy to disk.
🎯 rank.py
Loads the saved artifacts in 3.7 seconds, embeds the JD from the local model cache in 10.2 seconds, then runs the dot product across all 100K in 0.04 seconds. scorer.py computes the 5-component weighted score for every non-flagged candidate in 1.7 seconds. The top 100 are sorted, reasoning.py writes a fact-grounded note for each one, and submission.csv is saved.
🖥 demo_app/ (Gradio · HuggingFace Spaces)
Loads the top 1000 pre-screened features and embeddings, about 4 MB total. You paste any JD, hit rank, and get results in roughly 15 seconds with adjustable weight sliders. Runs on the free CPU Basic tier with no proprietary APIs.
⚙️ scorer.py: 5 components
skill_match: JD coverage times average proficiency and duration weight, plus a text boost and nice-to-have score. Keyword stuffers (non-AI title and under 12 months AI history) get 0.40x on this component.

career_quality: Starts at 0.5. Goes up for AI titles (+0.20), 4+ years in AI (+0.20), production experience (+0.12). Goes down for non-AI titles (-0.35), consulting-only backgrounds (-0.30), frequent title switching (-0.15).

availability: Open to work adds 0.20. Response rate (18%), interview rate (12%), notice period, location, GitHub score, and verification status all feed in.

education: Tier 1 (1.0), Tier 2 (0.75), Tier 3 (0.45), Tier 4 (0.20).
🍯 honeypot.py: 5 detection rules
1. Career date arithmetic is off by more than 12 months
2. Expert-level skill listed but zero months of usage
3. Total years of experience exceeds sum of role durations by more than 5 years
4. Overlapping jobs for more than 180 days
5. Five or more expert or advanced skills each with under 3 months used

65 profiles were detected and excluded before scoring

Technologies Used
🧠 sentence-transformers
all-MiniLM-L6-v2 is a 22 million parameter model producing 384-dimensional embeddings. We picked it because it runs entirely on CPU, handles a single JD embedding in about 10 seconds, and gives strong semantic representations for technical English text without needing a GPU anywhere in the pipeline.
⚡ NumPy matrix multiply
We replaced FAISS entirely at query time. Since embeddings are L2-normalised at precompute, a dot product is the same as cosine similarity. (100K x 384) @ (384x1) finishes in 0.04 seconds. This approach eliminates the recall loss that comes from approximate nearest-neighbour pre-filtering, which was cutting genuinely good candidates from our results.
📄 JSONL feature store
We switched from Python pickle to line-delimited JSON for the feature store. Loading 100,000 feature dictionaries from JSONL takes 3.7 seconds. The same data as a pickle file took 93 minutes. It is also human-readable, has no serialisation overhead, and works across Python versions without compatibility issues.
🔍 rapidfuzz
We use fuzzy partial-ratio matching to normalise skill names before scoring. Without this, "HuggingFace" and "Hugging Face Transformers" would be treated as two different skills. Same issue with "Open Search" vs "OpenSearch" and dozens of similar variations across 100,000 profiles. Fair skill scoring depends on getting this right.
🖥 Gradio + HuggingFace Spaces
A live interactive demo where you can paste any JD, adjust the weight sliders to shift what matters most, and see the top candidates in about 15 seconds. Deployed on the free CPU Basic tier. The top 1000 candidate features and embeddings ship as a 4 MB artifact bundle. No proprietary APIs, no API keys needed.
